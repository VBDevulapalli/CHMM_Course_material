## OSHA FOUNDATIONS

**OSH Act signed:** December 29, 1970

**Effective date:** April 28, 1971 — Workers Memorial Day

**Three agencies created:**
- OSHA — writes and enforces rules
- NIOSH — research only, sets RELs, cannot cite anyone
- OSHRC — hears appeals of contested citations

**OSHA covers:** Private sector, federal agencies

**OSHA does NOT cover:** Self-employed with no employees, family farm immediate family, mining (MSHA), nuclear (NRC)

**State Plans:** Must be at least as effective as federal OSHA — may be more stringent

**CFR location for OSHA:** 29 CFR

## HAZWOPER TRAINING LEVELS

| Level | Role | Min Hours | What They Do |
|-------|------|-----------|-------------|
| FRA | First Responder Awareness | Competency-based | Recognize, Retreat, Report only |
| FRO | First Responder Operations | 8 hours | Defensive — contain from perimeter |
| HMT | Hazmat Technician | 24 hours at FRO | Offensive — stops release at source |
| HMS | Hazmat Specialist | 24 hours at HMT | Technical support and agency liaison |
| IC | Incident Commander | 24 hours at FRO | Commands the entire scene |

**Annual refresher:** 8 hours for ALL levels FRO through IC

**Key distinction:** FRO = defensive only. HMT = offensive, goes to the source.

|-----------|--------------|--------------|-----------|

| Warm Zone | Contamination Reduction Zone | Decon station here | Intermediate |

**Movement rule:** Always Hot to Warm with decon, then Warm to Cold. Never skip decontamination.

## PPE LEVELS AT A GLANCE

**Level A — Maximum Protection**
- Fully encapsulating vapor-tight suit plus SCBA worn INSIDE the suit
- Use when: Unknown chemical, skin absorption hazard, IDLH with vapor risk
- Memory: Astronaut — totally sealed from environment

**Level B — Maximum Respiratory, Splash Skin Protection**
- Non-encapsulating suit plus SCBA worn OUTSIDE the suit
- Use when: IDLH atmosphere exists, skin absorption not primary concern
- Both A and B use SCBA — the suit type is the difference

**Level C — Air-Purifying Respirator Plus Splash Protection**
- APR or PAPR plus chemical-resistant coveralls and gloves
- Use when: O₂ normal AND contaminant identified AND below IDLH AND cartridge available
- Memory: Can breathe ambient air through filter

**Level D — Standard Work Clothing Only**
- Safety glasses, steel-toed boots, hard hat, work clothing
- Use when: No respiratory or significant skin hazard of any kind
- Memory: Daily work clothes

**Critical fact:** Higher letter equals LESS protection. A is maximum. D is minimum.

## CONFINED SPACE ATMOSPHERE TEST ORDER

Test in this exact order every time:

1. **OXYGEN** — Normal range is 19.5 to 23.5 percent
   - Below 19.5 percent = oxygen-deficient, entry prohibited without respiratory protection
   - Above 23.5 percent = oxygen-enriched, fire hazard, permit-required

2. **LEL / FLAMMABILITY** — Below 10 percent LEL is generally acceptable
   - Important: O₂ deficiency causes false low readings on many LEL instruments

3. **TOXIC CONTAMINANTS** — Compare to PEL, TLV, and IDLH for each substance

---

## CONFINED SPACE ROLES

**Entrant:** Goes inside. Must self-rescue immediately if conditions change.

**Attendant:** Stays OUTSIDE. Maintains entrant count. Communicates. NEVER enters the space even to rescue a collapsed worker. Activates retrieval system. Calls for rescue.

**Entry Supervisor:** Authorizes the permit. Cancels if conditions deteriorate.

## LOTO — SIX STEPS IN ORDER

1. Notify all affected employees
2. Identify all energy sources — electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, spring tension, gravity
3. Shut down equipment using normal stopping procedure
4. Isolate all energy sources — open disconnects, close valves
5. Apply personal locks — each worker places their own lock on each energy isolating device
6. Verify zero energy state — release stored energy, then try to start and confirm nothing happens

## INDUSTRIAL HYGIENE DEFINITION

**Anticipate — Recognize — Evaluate — Control**

This four-step sequence IS the definition of industrial hygiene. Know it cold.

---

## HIERARCHY OF CONTROLS — Best to Worst

1. Elimination — remove the hazard entirely
2. Substitution — replace with less hazardous alternative
3. Engineering Controls — ventilation, guards, enclosures — works regardless of behavior
4. Administrative Controls — rotation, procedures, training — depends on human behavior
5. PPE — last resort — only protects the individual wearing it correctly

## RESPIRATOR ASSIGNED PROTECTION FACTORS

| Respirator Type | APF | Maximum Use Concentration |
|----------------|-----|--------------------------|
| Half-face APR | 10 | 10 times the PEL |
| Full-face APR | 50 | 50 times the PEL |
| PAPR full-face | 1,000 | 1,000 times the PEL |
| SCBA positive pressure | 10,000 | 10,000 times the PEL |

MUC equals APF multiplied by PEL. If measured concentration exceeds MUC, upgrade the respirator.

**Level C conditions:** O₂ must be normal, contaminant must be identified, concentration must be below IDLH, appropriate cartridge must exist.

**Medical evaluation:** Required before first use of any respirator.

**Fit testing:** Before first use and annually thereafter.

## OSHA 300 LOG REQUIREMENTS

**Form 300:** Running log of recordable injuries and illnesses throughout the year

**Form 300A:** Annual summary — must be posted February 1 to April 30 every year

**Form 301:** Individual incident report for each recordable case

**Recordable conditions:** Death, days away from work, restricted duty, job transfer, medical treatment beyond first aid, loss of consciousness, significant injury or illness diagnosis.

**Not recordable:** First aid only — cleaning, bandaging, OTC medication at nonprescription dose.

**Who must keep 300 Log:** Employers with more than 10 employees in most industries.

## BIGGEST EXAM TRAPS

- FRO cannot approach the source to stop a release. FRO is defensive only. HMT goes to the source.
- Level A uses SCBA inside the suit. Level B uses SCBA outside the suit. Both use SCBA.
- Higher PPE letter equals LESS protection. A is maximum, D is minimum.
- The attendant NEVER enters the confined space even to rescue a collapsed worker.
- Oxygen must be tested before LEL — deficient O₂ causes false low LEL readings.
- Tagout is less safe than lockout. Lockout required whenever physically possible.
- Fatality = 8 hours. Hospitalization, amputation, eye loss = 24 hours.
- Citation contest period is 15 WORKING DAYS — not calendar days.
- OSHA enforces. NIOSH researches. NIOSH cannot cite anyone.
- PSM compliance audits = every 3 years. PHA revalidation = every 5 years. These are different schedules.
