## What Is a Hazardous Material?

**Exam Focus:**  
A “hazardous material” has multiple regulatory definitions depending on the agency and context (environmental, workplace, transportation, nuclear).

| Agency | Context | Key Definition |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **EPA (RCRA)** | Waste/environment | Substance that poses a threat by quantity, concentration, or characteristics; “hazardous waste” if discarded. Listed waste OR exhibits ignitability, reactivity, toxicity, corrosivity. [EPA-RCRA](https://www.epa.gov/rcra) |
| **DOT** | Transportation | Material posing “unreasonable risk to health, safety, and property” during transportation. Multiple triggers (hazmat, substances/wastes, marine pollutant...). [DOT Hazmat](https://hazmat.dot.gov/) |
| **OSHA** | Workplace | Chemical hazard in the workplace under HazCom (29 CFR 1910.1200). Health or physical hazard. [OSHA HazCom](https://www.osha.gov/hazcom) |
| **NRC**  | Nuclear | Radioactive materials, governed by Atomic Energy Act. Byproduct/source/special nuclear material. [NRC Overview](https://www.nrc.gov/about-nrc/radiation/protects-you/reg-matls.html) |

**Exam Tip:**  
You may get scenario questions where a material is regulated by one agency (e.g., DOT) but not another (e.g., RCRA/EPA). Know which definition triggers which regulatory program.

## Physical & Chemical Properties

Nearly every CHMM exam involves interpreting physical & chemical properties from a test report, SDS, or spill scenario.

### Flammability Properties

- **Flash Point:** Lowest temp where a liquid gives off enough vapor to ignite.  
  - <100°F (OSHA): Flammable
  - 100-200°F: Combustible (OSHA)
  - <60°C (140°F) (DOT): Flammable
- **Fire Point:** Slightly higher than flash point; temp where substance sustains combustion.
- **Autoignition Temp (AIT):** Substance self-ignites without spark/flame.

**Table: Common Flash Points**

| Chemical   | Flash Point (°C) | Hazard |
|------------|------------------|--------|
| Gasoline   | -43              | Extreme |
| Acetone    | -20              | High |
| Toluene    | 4                | High |

- **Flammable Range (Explosive Limits):**
  - **LEL/UEL:** Lower/Upper Explosive Limit (Methane LEL = 5%, UEL = 15%)
- **Vapor Density:** Compares weight of vapor to air (=1.0). >1 sinks, <1 rises.
- **Vapor Pressure:** Higher = more volatile; units mmHg.

### Toxicological Properties

- **LD50:** Single dose that kills 50% of a test population (mg/kg—lower is more toxic)
- **LC50:** Inhalation equivalent.
- **IDLH:** Immediately Dangerous to Life or Health (must escape in 30 min).
- **TLV/PEL/REL:** 
  - TLV = ACGIH Threshold Limit (advisory)
  - PEL = Legal limit (OSHA)
  - REL = NIOSH Recommended

**Routes of Exposure:** Inhalation, dermal, ingestion, injection.  
**Acute vs Chronic:** Acute—single/short exposure. Chronic—repeated/long exposure.

### Reactivity & Chemical Stability

- **Oxidizer:** Makes fires worse. Never store with flammables!
- **Water-Reactive:** Violent with water (e.g., sodium metal, CaC₂).
- **Pyrophoric:** Spontaneously ignites (<130°F)—extremely dangerous.
- **Corrosivity:** pH ≤2 or ≥12.5 or corrode steel >6.35mm/yr.

### Radioactive Materials (Brief)

- **Types:** Alpha (stopped by paper/skin), beta (aluminum), gamma (lead/concrete), neutron (concrete/water).
- **Units:** Curie, Becquerel, rad, rem, sievert.
- **Half-life:** Short = more hazardous for brief period, long = persistent.

## The GHS System and Safety Data Sheets (SDS)

**GHS (Globally Harmonized System)** — International system (adopted by OSHA) for classifying and labeling chemicals.

### GHS Container Labels — Must Include:
1. Product identifier
2. Signal word (“Danger”/“Warning”)
3. Hazard statement(s)
4. Precautionary statement(s)
5. Pictogram(s)
6. Supplier information

### SDS — 16 Required Sections (Know these for the exam!):
1. Identification
2. Hazard(s) identification
3. Composition/information on ingredients
4. First aid measures
5. Fire-fighting measures
6. Accidental release measures
7. Handling and storage
8. Exposure controls / personal protection
9. Physical and chemical properties
10. Stability and reactivity
11. Toxicological information
12. Ecological information
13. Disposal considerations
14. Transport information
15. Regulatory information
16. Other information

**OSHA Requires:** Sections 1-11 & 16 to be completed. (12-15 not enforced but must appear.)

### GHS Pictograms
- Flame: Flammable, self-reactives, pyrophorics
- Flame over circle: Oxidizer
- Exploding bomb: Explosive, organic peroxide
- Skull: Acute toxicity
- Exclamation: Irritant
- Health hazard: Carcinogen, mutagen
- Corrosion: Corrosive to skin/eyes/metals
- Gas cylinder: Gases under pressure
- Environment: Aquatic toxicity (not OSHA-enforced in US)

**More:** [OSHA HazCom GHS Info](https://www.osha.gov/hazcom/ghs.html)

## Regulatory Ecosystem — Who Does What

**EPA:**  
- Main law: RCRA (cradle to grave)
- Also: CERCLA (Superfund), TSCA, EPCRA, CAA, CWA

**OSHA:**  
- Worker safety (HazCom, HAZWOPER, PSM, Respiratory, Confined Space)

**DOT:**  
- Transport—classification, packaging, labeling, placarding, shipping papers. (49 CFR, PHMSA, FMCSA, FAA)

**NRC:**  
- Radioactive material for civilian use (not DOE—military).

**ATSDR:**  
- Public health, toxic exposures, MRLs, Toxic Profiles (CDC, HHS).

**Exam Note:**  
If a scenario involves reporting a release, remember the **National Response Center:** 1-800-424-8802 (CERCLA RQ, CWA, many EPA notifications)

## Hazard Classification Systems

### DOT Hazard Classes (49 CFR 172.101)

| Class | Name                                           |
|-------|------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | Explosives (1.1–1.6)                          |
| 2     | Gases (2.1 Flammable, 2.2 Non-flammable, 2.3 Toxic) |
| 3     | Flammable & Combustible Liquids               |
| 4     | Flammable Solids (4.1, 4.2, 4.3)              |
| 5     | Oxidizers (5.1), Organic Peroxides (5.2)      |
| 6     | Toxic (6.1), Infectious (6.2)                 |
| 7     | Radioactive                                   |
| 8     | Corrosive                                     |
| 9     | Miscellaneous                                 |

- **Packing Groups:** I (great danger), II (medium danger), III (minor)

### NFPA 704 (Fire Diamond)
- **Blue:** Health (0–4)
- **Red:** Flammability (0–4)
- **Yellow:** Reactivity (0–4)
- **White:** Special hazards (`W̄` = water reactive, `OX` = oxidizer)

### RCRA Hazardous Waste Characteristics (IRTC)

- **D001:** Ignitability (flash point < 60°C)
- **D002:** Corrosivity (pH ≤2 or ≥12.5 or corrodes steel >6.35mm/yr)
- **D003:** Reactivity (violent, water-reactive, explosive, toxic gas gen.)
- **D004–D043:** Toxicity (fails TCLP)

**TCLP Metals Limits (mg/L):**
- Lead: 5.0
- Arsenic: 5.0
- Mercury: 0.2
- Chromium: 5.0
- Cadmium: 1.0
- Benzene: 0.5
- Vinyl chloride: 0.2

## Pollution Prevention Hierarchy

**EPA's Order of Preference**
1. Source Reduction (BEST—prevent at source)
2. Recycling/Recovery
3. Treatment
4. Disposal (LAST RESORT)

[Source — EPA P2](https://www.epa.gov/p2/learn-about-pollution-prevention)

## Environmental Fate of Chemicals

- **Henry’s Law Constant:** Volatilization from water to air (high = more likely to become vapor)
- **Koc:** Sorption to soil (high = sticks to soil, low = mobile in groundwater)
- **Log Kow:** Partitioning to fat (bioaccumulation potential: high = more accumulation)
    - DDT, PCBs: Among highest bioaccumulation and persistence
- **Bioaccumulation:** Builds up in organisms
- **Biomagnification:** Increases up the food chain (e.g., DDT, methylmercury)
- **Persistence:** How long chemical survives before breaking down (POPs: PCBs, dioxins)

## Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP)

- **Required by many states (e.g., California) for facilities storing threshold hazmat quantities**
- Includes: Facility info, emergency plan, site map, hazmat inventory, equipment, training
- **Federal:** EPCRA Section 312 (Tier I/Tier II reports). Feeds to SERCs/LEPCs.

## Memorize by Heart List

- **Flash point:** Lowest temp vapor ignites (not sustained)
- **Fire point:** Sustained ignition (higher than flash point)
- **AIT:** Self-ignition, no spark
- **LEL/UEL:** Min/max % in air for ignition (methane LEL 5%, UEL 15%)
- **Vapor density:** >1 sinks (propane), <1 rises (methane)
- **LD50 = dose kills 50% (mg/kg); LC50 = inhalation route**
- **IDLH = max safe for brief escape (NIOSH)**
- **TLV-TWA (8hr), TLV-STEL (15min), TLV-C (never exceed) = ACGIH; PEL = OSHA**
- **SDS section 2 = hazard ID; section 8 = controls/PPE**
- **DOT classes 1–9 (get the numbers and symbols memorized)**
- **Packing Group I = greatest danger**
- **EPA: RCRA, CERCLA, TSCA, EPCRA; OSHA: HazCom, HAZWOPER, PSM; DOT: 49 CFR; NRC: radioactive; ATSDR: toxic exposure**
- **P2 hierarchy: Source reduction, recycling, treatment, disposal**
- **TCLP critical values – lead, arsenic, mercury, benzene**
- **National Response Center = 1-800-424-8802**
